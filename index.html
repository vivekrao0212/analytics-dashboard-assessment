Different types of electric vehicle population

BEVs are fully electric vehicles powered solely by batteries, with no internal combustion engine. These vehicles produce zero tailpipe emissions.

PHEVs are vehicles that have both an internal combustion engine and an electric battery. They can run on electric power alone for short distances and switch to gasoline for longer trips, making them more flexible than BEVs.

Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle Eligiblity

Clean Alternative Fuel Vehicle (CAFV) eligibility refers to the qualification of vehicles to be recognized as clean fuel vehicles that use alternative, non-petroleum-based fuels. These vehicles often qualify for various incentives, tax credits, or exemptions designed to promote the adoption of greener transportation options. The eligibility criteria for CAFVs depend on the specific state or federal programs and the type of alternative fuel the vehicle uses.
